Demonstration of General Accessibility Models: Learning Accessibility Needs from Everyday Computer Use

Yuxuan Liu, Ruijie Zheng, Brandon Kim, Jason Wu, Anhong Guo

We present the General Accessibility Model (GAM), an architecture that learns user accessibility needs by observing everyday computer use. From screen recordings and input streams, GAM generates confidence-weighted propositions describing a user's interaction strategies and in-situ accessibility barriers, and periodically reflects on observations to revise them — turning everyday interaction into a continuously updated source of knowledge for personalized accessibility support.
